Frequently asked questions — Website and conversion for brokers

How can a real estate broker reduce missed showings?
Five web levers make the difference: online showing booking, automated SMS and email reminders, active confirmation, a short buyer pre-qualification at booking, and grouping showings or holding open houses with online registration. Together, they capture the right contact, qualify intent and strongly reduce wasted trips, within Law 25 and OACIQ rules.
Why does a broker website get visits but few requests?
Traffic and conversion are two separate problems. In real estate, the site must serve two audiences with opposite intentions: the seller who wants a valuation and the buyer who wants to see listings and book a showing. If the site does not clearly separate these two paths and offer an obvious action to each — "Estimate my property" and "Book a showing" — both leave.
Does a free online valuation help generate listings?
Yes, it is one of the best magnets for seller prospects. Many homeowners begin by searching for the value of their home. A simple, confidential valuation request captures that prospect early, before they contact a competitor. The form must stay short, secure and Law 25 compliant, and communication compliant with OACIQ rules.
