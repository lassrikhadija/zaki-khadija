Frequently asked questions from Quebec lawyers

Local Pack & Visibility

Why is my law firm invisible on Google even though I have a website?

Having a website does not guarantee visibility in Google's Local Pack — the block of 3 results displayed first when a client searches "divorce lawyer Montreal" or "immigration lawyer Plateau". That block is driven by your Google Business Profile, not your website. If your listing is incomplete, lacks recent reviews or is poorly categorized, you are absent from those results. Your competitor with a better listing captures the client before they ever discover your expertise.

How long does it take to appear in Google's local top 3 for a law firm?

For firms starting from a low baseline, the first ranking improvements are typically visible within a few weeks after Google Business Profile optimization. Reaching a stable top-3 position in a competitive neighbourhood like Plateau, Rosemont or downtown Montreal generally takes several months of sustained work. Results depend on competition levels in your practice area, your volume of recent reviews, and the quality of your Schema markup. Our audit will give you an honest estimate based on your specific situation.

My firm is already on Google Maps — is that enough to attract clients?

Being present on Google Maps is only the first step of a much longer optimization journey. A well-optimized Google Business Profile for a law firm includes precise legal categories, listed practice areas, recent professional photos, regular posts, and — most importantly — a steady stream of recent reviews with professional responses. The majority of firms we audit have their profile filled in at less than 40%, which directly penalizes their Local Pack ranking.

Is local SEO really useful for a boutique firm or solo practitioner?

Local SEO is particularly advantageous for boutique firms and solo practitioners because it levels the playing field against large firms. Google does not favour firm size — it favours relevance, proximity and local prominence. A solo lawyer with an optimized Google Business Profile, recent reviews and pages by practice area and neighbourhood can significantly outrank a large firm that has neglected its local digital presence.

Reviews & Professional Ethics

Are Quebec lawyers allowed to solicit Google Reviews?

Yes, provided it complies with the Code of Professional Conduct of the Barreau du Quebec. You cannot selectively target only satisfied clients, offer an incentive for a review, or write testimonials yourself. However, you may mention verbally or by email after a mandate that Google reviews matter to your firm and provide a direct link. Our method is fully compliant: non-selective automated follow-up, no pressure, no incentive, designed specifically for the ethical constraints of regulated professions.

A former client left a negative Google review — what should I do?

Never ignore it, and never respond emotionally or by disclosing confidential case information. Respond within 24 to 48 hours with a professional, brief reply: thank the client for their feedback, express regret that the experience did not meet your standards, and invite them to contact you directly. Your response must respect professional confidentiality — never go into the specifics of the matter. A negative review paired with an exemplary professional response often inspires more trust than a perfect record with no critical feedback. We provide response templates adapted to the ethical constraints of the Barreau.

How many Google reviews are needed to rank in the legal Local Pack?

There is no fixed threshold, but in an average Montreal market, firms in the top 3 generally have a significant number of reviews with a rating between 4.6 and 5.0. The recency of reviews matters as much as the total volume: a firm with many old reviews will be disadvantaged compared to a competitor with fewer but all recent ones. Our system maintains a regular, Barreau-compliant flow of new reviews without requiring ongoing effort from you.

AI & Technology

Can ChatGPT actually recommend my law firm?

Yes, and it is happening with increasing frequency. When a client asks ChatGPT to "recommend a business lawyer in Montreal", the AI draws its answers from structured data, directory mentions, articles and Schema.org markup. Firms with complete Schema LegalService markup, rich practice area descriptions and consistent web mentions are cited considerably more often in these responses. The considerable majority of Quebec law firms have not yet taken advantage of this opportunity.

What is Schema LegalService markup and how is it installed?

Schema LegalService is a JSON-LD markup standard based on Schema.org that describes your firm in a structured way for search engines and AI systems. It specifies your practice areas (family law, criminal law, immigration, business law), languages of service, fee structure, exact address, hours and contact information. It is installed in your website's HTML and is invisible to visitors. The considerable majority of Quebec legal websites either lack this markup entirely or have it incorrectly implemented — making them invisible to generative AI systems.

Does Quebec's Law 25 apply to my law firm's website?

Yes, without exception. As a law firm, your Law 25 compliance is particularly scrutinized. The law applies to any website collecting personal information from Quebec residents — including your contact forms, consultation request forms and analytics cookies. Your obligations include: an accessible privacy policy, a cookie consent banner, a process for handling access and correction requests, and designating a person responsible for the protection of personal information. Our audit verifies your compliance across all of these requirements.

We recommend a minimum monthly cadence: Google Business Profile posts, responses to new reviews, updates for holidays and office closures, and ranking position analysis.
