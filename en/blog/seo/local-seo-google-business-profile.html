Local SEO & Google Business Profile: Complete Setup Guide 2026

Khadija AitLassri April 28, 2026 11 min read

30-Second Summary

  • Google Business Profile (GBP) is the #1 local SEO lever for service-based Canadian businesses. It's free and drives real foot traffic and calls. According to Think with Google, 76% of people who search locally visit a business within 24 hours.
  • 7-step setup: claim, verify, complete NAP, select primary category + subcategories, add services + attributes, 10+ photos, set hours and service areas.
  • Reviews are the strongest local ranking signal. According to BrightLocal 2024, profiles with 10+ photos receive 42% more direction requests and calls.
  • Weekly Google Posts + monthly audit = sustained local visibility advantage over competitors who set it up and forget it.

Why Google Business Profile Is Your #1 Local Channel

When someone in Montreal searches "plumber near me" or "accountant Plateau-Mont-Royal", Google shows a local pack — a map with 3 highlighted local businesses — before any organic results. These 3 spots capture the majority of clicks on local searches.

Google Business Profile is what determines whether you appear in those 3 spots. Your website ranking matters, but for local searches, GBP is the primary factor. A business with a poorly filled profile and no reviews, regardless of how good its website is, will rarely crack the local pack for competitive searches.

For Canadian service businesses — contractors, lawyers, accountants, restaurants, clinics, agencies — GBP is often the single highest-ROI marketing investment available. It is completely free to set up and maintain.

The 7-Step Google Business Profile Setup

Step 1 — Claim Your Profile

Go to business.google.com and search for your business name. If it already exists (Google may have created an auto-generated listing), claim it. If not, create it from scratch. Claiming prevents competitors or bad actors from editing your information.

Step 2 — Verify Your Listing

Google requires verification before your profile becomes live. In 2026, the most common method is video verification: record a short walk-through of your business premises showing the address, signage, and work environment. Postcard verification (5-14 business days) remains available. Complete verification promptly — an unverified profile has zero ranking power.

Step 3 — Complete Your NAP Exactly

NAP = Name, Address, Phone. These must be identical — character for character — across your GBP, your website's Contact page, and every online directory (Yellow Pages, Yelp, Bing Places, Facebook). NAP inconsistency is one of the most common local SEO killers. "123 rue Sainte-Catherine" and "123, Ste-Catherine St" are different — standardize to one format.

Step 4 — Select Primary Category + Subcategories

Your primary business category is the most important GBP ranking signal. Choose the most specific, accurate category available — not the most generic. Example: "Web Design Company" is better than "Company". Add up to 9 secondary subcategories for all your service verticals. Research what categories your top local competitors use by viewing their GBP profiles.

Step 5 — Add All Services, Products, and Attributes

Fill in every service you offer with descriptions. Add products if applicable. Select all relevant attributes: "women-led", "wheelchair accessible", "online appointments available", "serves the Laurentians", "free parking" — Google uses attributes to match your profile to detailed local queries like "accessible dentist Montreal north".

Step 6 — Upload 10+ High-Quality Photos

According to BrightLocal 2024, profiles with 10 or more photos receive 42% more direction requests and calls than profiles with fewer than 3 photos. Add: exterior photo (matching your street address), interior photos, team photos, service-in-action photos, logo, and cover image. Update photos every quarter — recency matters.

Step 7 — Set Hours, Holiday Hours, and Service Areas

Set accurate hours including holiday exceptions. For service-area businesses (no fixed public address), hide the physical address and define service areas by city or postal code. Inaccurate hours generate negative reviews and hurt trust signals. Enable the messaging feature if you can respond within a few hours.

The Reviews Strategy: Your Most Powerful Local Signal

Google Reviews are the strongest local ranking signal after proximity and category relevance. A business with 80 reviews at 4.6 stars will consistently outrank a competitor with 12 reviews at 4.9 stars for most queries.

How to collect reviews ethically and systematically:

  • Send a follow-up email within 24 hours of service completion with a direct GBP review link (get your link from your GBP dashboard → "Get more reviews").
  • Add the review link to your email signature.
  • Train your team to ask satisfied clients in person: "If you were happy with the work, a Google review helps us a lot."
  • Never offer compensation for reviews — Google can suspend profiles for this.

Always respond to reviews — especially negative ones. A professional, empathetic response to a negative review builds more trust than 5 unanswered positive reviews. Response template for negative reviews: acknowledge the experience, apologize for the frustration, offer to resolve it offline with a direct contact method.

Google Posts: Weekly Content for Local Visibility

Google Posts are short updates (up to 1,500 characters, optional image) that appear directly on your GBP listing in search results. Regular posts signal to Google that your profile is actively managed — a key ranking factor in the local algorithm.

5 post types to use:

  • What's New: share useful tips, news, or behind-the-scenes content weekly.
  • Offers: promote seasonal discounts or referral programs with an expiry date.
  • Events: webinars, open houses, community participation.
  • Products: highlight specific services with pricing and photos.
  • Q&A seeding: ask and answer your own most common questions — these appear prominently on your profile and catch search intent.

Recommended cadence: one post per week. Posts expire after 7 days (Offers expire on your set date) — maintain freshness with consistent weekly publishing.

Monthly and Quarterly Maintenance Rituals

Monthly (30 minutes)

  • 1Check and respond to all new reviews (positive and negative).
  • 2Publish 4 new Google Posts (one per week).
  • 3Review GBP Insights: how many calls, direction requests, website clicks? Track month over month.
  • 4Send review request emails to the month's satisfied clients.

Quarterly (60 minutes)

  • 1Add 3-5 fresh photos (seasonal, team, recent projects).
  • 2Review and update your services list — add any new offerings, remove discontinued ones.
  • 3Update holiday hours for the upcoming quarter.
  • 4Check NAP consistency — verify your address and phone appear identically on your website, Facebook, and top directories.
