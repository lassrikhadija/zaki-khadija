A Competitor Just Opened Near Your Clinic: What to Do?

Khadija AitLassri June 11, 2026 9 min read

30-second summary

  • The wrong reaction: price war and panic. They erode your margins without solving the real issue.
  • First audit your real local position before reacting.
  • Your fast defences: Google listing + reviews, then your difference (tenure, relationship).
  • The best bulwark: retain your current patients — it costs far less to keep one than to acquire one.
The key idea A new clinic starts from zero on online reputation. Your edge — accumulated reviews, tenure, loyal patients — is exactly what it doesn't have yet. The right strategy is to lean on it, not to slash your prices.

A new dental clinic opening a few streets away is a legitimate worry. But panic is a poor advisor. With a little method, an established clinic almost always has the edge — provided it activates the right levers, in the right order. Here's how to respond calmly.

The wrong reaction (to avoid)

Two reflexes cost dearly:

  • The price war. Cutting your fees erodes profitability without solving the real issue — visibility and trust. Most patients don't choose on the lowest price.
  • Disparaging the competitor. It always backfires, and can raise issues under the Order's rules. Winning differentiation is positive: show your value, don't attack the other.

Step 1 — Audit your local position

Before reacting, measure. Where do you appear when someone searches for a dentist in your area? Are your Google listing and the local pack to your advantage or the newcomer's? How many reviews do you have, and how fresh? This objective snapshot avoids panicking over nothing — or ignoring a real decline. You only fix well what you've first measured.

Step 2 — Lock down your Google listing and reviews

This is your fastest and cheapest defence. A complete, up-to-date Google listing with real photos and recent reviews weighs heavily in the local pack and in trust — especially against a newcomer with no reviews yet. Choosing a dentist is a trust decision: 87% of consumers read reviews before choosing a professional (BrightLocal 2024).

Step 3 — Lean on your difference

Bet on what a new clinic doesn't have yet: your tenure, your accumulated reviews, the trust relationship with your patients, your proven expertise. Highlight it honestly on your site and listing — exactly what E-E-A-T values. A new clinic starts from zero on reputation: that's your structural advantage.

Step 4 — Retain your current patients

The immediate risk isn't only capturing fewer new patients: it's seeing existing patients leave for the novelty. Yet retaining costs far less than acquiring. Strengthen the experience, appointment reminders and reactivation of inactive patients. A loyal base is your best bulwark — and the source of your next referrals.

Special case: a chain or franchise with a big budget

If the newcomer is a large brand with a high ad budget, don't play on its turf (volume). Play on yours: human proximity, the relationship, local expertise and the reputation that years have built. A chain can buy visibility; it can't buy your review history or your patients' trust. Focus your efforts where you're unbeatable.
