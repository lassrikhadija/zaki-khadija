Frequently asked questions from Quebec restaurant owners

Local Pack & Visibility

Why is my restaurant impossible to find on Google even though I have a website and a Google listing?

Having a website is not enough to appear in Google's Local Pack — the block of three addresses with a map displayed first when a diner searches "restaurant Plateau" or "brunch near me". That block is driven by your Google Business Profile, not your website. If your listing lacks recent photos, reviews or the right cuisine category, you do not appear there — and the diner books the table next door before ever seeing your menu.

How long does it take for a restaurant to appear in Google's local top 3?

Starting from a nearly empty listing, the first visibility gains become noticeable after a few weeks of work on your Google Business Profile. Holding a top-3 spot in a hotly contested area — Plateau, Mile-End, Old Montreal — generally takes several months of steady effort. The pace depends on the density of restaurants around you, how often you collect new reviews, and the quality of your photos. Our audit gives you a realistic estimate specific to your neighbourhood.

My listing is already on Google Maps — is that enough to fill my dining room?

Being present on Google Maps is only the starting point. A listing that fills a dining room includes the right cuisine category, an up-to-date menu, a reservation link, appetizing photos of dishes and room, regular posts, and — most importantly — a steady stream of fresh reviews with thoughtful responses. Most of the listings we audit are barely half-filled, which directly drags down their Local Pack ranking.

Is local SEO really useful for a small restaurant or neighbourhood café?

That is exactly where it pays off most. Local SEO levels the playing field for the small spot against the chains, because Google does not reward the size of the brand: it values relevance, proximity and local prominence. An independent café with an optimized listing, recent reviews, great photos and pages by neighbourhood can outrank a banner that has neglected its local digital presence.

Reviews & Reputation

Can a restaurant ask its customers for Google Reviews?

Yes, and it is strongly recommended: unlike regulated professions, a restaurant can freely invite its customers to leave a review. The only limit is Google's policy: no fake reviews, no purchased reviews and no incentive (a discount for a comment). In practice, you ask at the right moment — at the end of the meal, on the receipt or via a discreet QR code on the table — and you respond to every review. Our method builds this habit without slowing down service.

A customer left a negative Google review on my restaurant — what should I do?

Never ignore the review and never respond in the heat of the moment. Within a day or two, post a short, courteous reply: thank them for visiting, acknowledge the point raised without over-justifying, and invite them back. A composed reply to a criticism often reassures future customers more than a string of five-star reviews with no responses. We provide response templates adapted to the restaurant business.

How many reviews and photos are needed to stand out in the Local Pack?

There is no fixed threshold. Recency matters as much as quantity: a listing with many old reviews and photos is at a disadvantage against a restaurant that keeps receiving new ones. What counts is a constant flow of credible reviews, systematic responses, and recent, appetizing photos. Our system keeps this rhythm going without weighing down your service.

AI & Technology

Can ChatGPT actually recommend my restaurant?

Yes, and it is happening more and more often. When a diner asks ChatGPT "a good Italian spot in the Mile-End", the assistant builds its answer from structured data, directory mentions, articles and Schema.org markup. Restaurants with complete Schema Restaurant markup, a described menu and consistent web mentions stand out considerably more often. It is a space most Quebec restaurants do not occupy yet.

What is Schema Restaurant markup and how is it installed?

Schema Restaurant is a JSON-LD markup from Schema.org that describes your establishment in a structured way for search engines and AI: cuisine type, menu, price range, reservation option, hours and exact address. It is installed in your website's HTML and is invisible to visitors. Most Quebec restaurant websites either lack it or have it incorrectly implemented — making them invisible to generative AI.

Does Quebec's Law 25 apply to my restaurant's website?

Yes, as soon as your site collects personal information from Quebec residents — online reservation, contact form, newsletter sign-up, takeout order, analytics cookies. Your obligations: an accessible privacy policy, a cookie consent banner, a process for handling access and correction requests, and designating a person responsible for the protection of personal information. Our audit verifies each of these points.

Do I need to rebuild my entire website to improve my local SEO?

Not necessarily. Most local SEO gains do not require a complete rebuild. Google Business Profile optimization, reviews, Schema Restaurant markup and NAP citations can all be handled independently of your current site. That said, if your site displays poorly on mobile, loads slowly, has no readable menu or no pages by neighbourhood, a partial or full rebuild becomes worthwhile. Our audit tells you exactly what is priority to maximize your return without unnecessary spending.

How long do local SEO optimizations need to be maintained for a restaurant?

Local SEO is not a one-time project — it is ongoing maintenance. Google updates its algorithms regularly, your competitors improve their presence each month, and diners look for recent reviews and photos rather than ones from years ago. We recommend a minimum monthly cadence: Google Business Profile posts, responses to new reviews, updates for holidays and hours, and ranking analysis. Restaurants that sustain their optimizations consistently dominate their local market over the long term.
