Specialty Pages for Lawyers: Dominate "Divorce Lawyer Montreal", "Immigration Lawyer Rosemont"

Khadija AitLassri June 2, 2026 10 min read

30-Second Summary

  • A law firm's homepage cannot rank for every combination of practice area and neighbourhood. Specialty and neighbourhood pages solve this by creating dedicated pages for each high-intent local query.
  • A page targeting "Divorce Lawyer Rosemont" captures clients who are ready to call — these are among the highest-intent searches in local legal SEO.
  • The critical rule: each page must have genuinely unique content. Duplicate pages with only the neighbourhood name changed are penalized by Google and provide no value to clients.
  • A firm with 5 well-built specialty pages can rank for 5 different high-intent queries simultaneously — effectively multiplying its local visibility without additional advertising spend.

Why Your Homepage Cannot Do Everything

A law firm's homepage typically targets its own name, the city, and perhaps one or two main practice areas. That covers a fraction of how prospective clients actually search. Someone in Rosemont facing a divorce does not search for "law firm Montreal" — they search for "divorce lawyer Rosemont" or "family law attorney near me". A homepage cannot rank for this level of specificity.

Specialty and neighbourhood pages solve this directly. Each page is a dedicated, focused entry point for a specific combination of: practice area + neighbourhood. A firm with 5 such pages can rank simultaneously for 5 different high-intent local queries, each attracting clients from a different geographic area or with a different legal need. This is one of the most efficient local SEO investments for a law firm.

What Makes a Good Specialty Page

A specialty page for a law firm is not simply your services page with a neighbourhood name inserted. It is a standalone page with unique, genuinely useful content for a specific prospective client in a specific area. Google's quality guidelines are explicit: pages with near-duplicate content that differ only in location names are considered thin or spam content and will not rank well.

The elements of a high-quality specialty page for a law firm include:

  • H1 with practice area + neighbourhood: e.g., "Divorce Lawyer in Rosemont, Montreal — [Firm Name]"
  • Unique introduction: Who is this page for? What specific situation might bring someone in that neighbourhood to need this practice area?
  • Neighbourhood context: Demographic characteristics relevant to the legal need (e.g., proportion of families with children for family law, immigrant population for immigration law)
  • Specific services in that practice area: Not a generic list, but the services most relevant to clients in that neighbourhood
  • Practical local information: Proximity to family courts, immigration offices, or relevant government offices near that neighbourhood
  • A clear CTA: Phone number, consultation booking form, or both
  • Schema LegalService markup: With the areaServed property set to the neighbourhood

Choosing Your Priority Combinations

Not all practice area + neighbourhood combinations are worth a dedicated page. Prioritize based on two factors: the volume of searches for that combination, and the commercial value of the cases it generates.

High-priority practice areas for specialty pages

  • Family law / Divorce: High search volume, immediate need, clients are geographically close to a courthouse
  • Immigration law: Very geo-specific need, clients often search in their own neighbourhood or near their IRCC office
  • Labour and employment law: Common need across all urban neighbourhoods, especially in areas with industrial or service-sector employment concentration
  • Real estate law: Hyperlocal by nature — clients almost always want a lawyer near the property they are transacting on
  • Criminal defence: Urgent, high-intent searches by location and court proximity

High-priority neighbourhoods in Montreal

Plateau-Mont-Royal, Rosemont–La Petite-Patrie, Villeray, Ahuntsic, Verdun, CDN-NDG, Saint-Laurent, Laval, Longueuil, Brossard. Each of these has distinct demographic profiles that influence the mix of legal services most in demand.

Avoiding the Duplicate Content Trap

The most common mistake when building specialty pages is creating 10 nearly identical pages with a find-and-replace on the neighbourhood name. This strategy does not work. Google identifies this pattern as thin or manipulative content and will either ignore these pages entirely or suppress the entire domain's rankings as a result.

The solution is to write each page from a genuinely different angle. Research the neighbourhood: its population demographics, proximity to relevant courts and government offices, types of employment (relevant for labour law), housing market characteristics (relevant for real estate law), and immigration profile (relevant for immigration law). This research takes time but produces pages that Google will rank and that clients will actually find useful.

A practical test Read your two most similar specialty pages side by side. If you can swap the neighbourhood name between them without the content becoming noticeably wrong or awkward, the pages are too similar. Add at least 200 words of genuinely neighbourhood-specific information to each before publishing.

Internal Linking: The Structure That Multiplies Authority

Specialty pages do not work in isolation. Their ranking power depends significantly on how they are connected to the rest of your website. The recommended internal linking structure for a law firm is:

  • Your main practice area page (e.g., /family-law/) links to each of its neighbourhood sub-pages (/family-law/rosemont/, /family-law/plateau/, etc.)
  • Each neighbourhood sub-page links back to the main practice area page and to the contact/consultation page
  • Related practice area pages link to each other (e.g., family law links to divorce law, immigration law links to refugee law)
  • Your homepage links to your main practice area pages

This creates a clear silo structure that concentrates topical authority. Google can clearly understand your firm's expertise in each area, and the authority of your strongest pages flows to your newer, less-established specialty pages through these links.

Frequently Asked Questions

A specialty or neighbourhood page is a dedicated page targeting a specific combination of practice area and geography — for example, "Divorce Lawyer Rosemont" or "Immigration Lawyer Montreal Nord". These pages allow a firm to rank for high-intent local searches that a generic homepage cannot capture.

Start with 3 to 5 pages targeting your highest-priority combinations of practice area and neighbourhood. Prioritize by search volume and commercial value. Each page requires unique, genuinely useful content — do not create more pages than you can populate with distinct, valuable information.

An effective specialty page includes: an H1 with the practice area and neighbourhood, a unique introduction for that specific client, neighbourhood demographic context relevant to the legal need, specific services offered, practical local information, a clear CTA, and Schema LegalService markup with the areaServed property.

Only if the pages have duplicate or near-duplicate content. Google penalizes pages that are nearly identical with only the location name changed. Each page must have unique, genuinely different content with neighbourhood-specific context. If you cannot write meaningfully different content for a page, do not create it.

Each specialty page should link back to the main practice area page, to the contact page, and to related specialty pages. The main practice area page should link to each of its neighbourhood sub-pages. This silo structure concentrates topical authority and allows each page to benefit from the authority of the others.

For new pages on an established domain, expect initial ranking signals within a few weeks and competitive rankings within 2 to 4 months for moderate-competition queries. For highly competitive queries like "divorce lawyer downtown Montreal", allow 4 to 6 months of consistent effort, including building internal links and collecting relevant reviews mentioning that neighbourhood.
