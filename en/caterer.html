The method

The NEXTIWEB method for caterers

6 levers we audit, activate and keep running for you

Google Business Profile

Profile completed in full: the right service category, up-to-date packages, price range, quote-request link, exact service area, mouth-watering photos of your buffets and receptions, and regular posts (seasonal menus, events served) to signal your activity to Google.

Google Reviews

A simple review strategy compliant with Google's policy: ask at the right moment (thank-you email after the event, final invoice), with no fake reviews and no incentive, and respond to every comment. Build a stream of fresh reviews that reassures future planners and feeds your local ranking.

Event-Type & Neighbourhood Pages

Dedicated pages for "wedding caterer Laval", "corporate catering downtown", "reception catering South Shore" with local content, service type and proximity signals. They attract planners ready to request a quote.

Schema FoodEstablishment

Complete JSON-LD markup (FoodEstablishment type: cuisine type, packages, price range, ordering option, service area, contact details). This is what ChatGPT, Perplexity and Google AI read to suggest a caterer. Nearly absent from Quebec caterer websites.

NAP Citations

Your Name, Address and Phone number kept consistent across Quebec directories and platforms (PagesJaunes, Yelp, wedding and event directories, quote platforms). The slightest mismatch between two directories weakens your local signals.

Structured FAQ for AI

A FAQ and your menus marked up with FAQPage and Menu Schema, integrated into your site. When a client asks ChatGPT "a vegetarian caterer for an office party in Montreal", the AI draws from this data and cites your service. Caterers without markup stay out of these high-intent conversations.

Frequently asked questions

Frequently asked questions from Quebec caterers

Local Pack & Visibility

Having a website is not enough to appear in Google's Local Pack — the block of three addresses with a map displayed first when someone searches "wedding caterer Montreal" or "corporate catering near me". It is your Google Business Profile that feeds that block, not your website. If your listing lacks recent buffet photos, reviews or the right service category, you do not appear there — and the planner requests a quote from a competitor before ever seeing your menus.

From a nearly empty listing, the first signs of visibility show up after a few weeks of work on your Google Business Profile. Holding a top-3 spot on a hotly contested query — "wedding caterer Montreal", "event catering Laval" — generally takes several months of steady effort. The pace depends on how many caterers cover your service area, how often you collect new reviews, and the quality of your event photos. Our audit gives you a realistic estimate specific to your market.

Showing up on Google Maps is only a first step. A listing that generates quote requests includes the right service category, up-to-date packages, a quote-request link, delivery and service-area info, appetizing photos of buffets and receptions, regular posts, and — most importantly — a steady stream of fresh reviews with thoughtful responses. Most of the caterer listings we audit are half-filled, which directly drags down their Local Pack ranking.

That is precisely where it pays off most. Local SEO levels the playing field for the small caterer against the large event companies, because Google does not reward the size of the brand: it values relevance, proximity and local prominence. An independent caterer with an optimized listing, recent reviews, great photos and pages by event type can outrank an established player that has neglected its local digital presence.

Reviews & Reputation

Yes, and it is strongly recommended: unlike regulated professions, a caterer can freely invite its customers to leave a review. One rule applies, Google's: no fake reviews, no bought reviews, no discount traded for a comment. In practice, you ask at the right moment — a few days after the event, in the thank-you email or on the final invoice — and you respond to every review. Our method builds this habit without adding to your client follow-up.

Never leave a review unanswered, and never reply in the heat of the moment. Within a day or two, post a short, courteous reply: thank them for the trust placed in you for their event, acknowledge the point raised without over-justifying, and offer to discuss it directly. A composed reply to a criticism often reassures future planners more than a string of five-star reviews with no responses. We provide response templates adapted to the catering business.

There is no precise cut-off. Recency matters as much as quantity: a listing with many old reviews and photos is at a disadvantage against a caterer that keeps receiving new ones. What counts is a constant flow of credible reviews, systematic responses, and recent, appetizing photos of your receptions. Our system keeps this rhythm going without weighing down your client follow-up.

AI & Technology

When a client asks ChatGPT "a caterer for an 80-person wedding in Montreal", the assistant builds its answer from structured data, directory mentions, articles and Schema.org markup. Caterers with complete Schema FoodEstablishment markup, described packages and consistent web mentions stand out considerably more often. It is a space most Quebec caterers do not occupy yet.

Schema FoodEstablishment is a JSON-LD markup from Schema.org that describes your service in a structured way for search engines and AI: cuisine type, packages, price range, service area, ordering option, hours and exact contact details. It is embedded directly in your pages' HTML and stays invisible to visitors. Most Quebec caterer websites either lack it or have it incorrectly implemented — making them invisible to generative AI.
