Frequently asked questions from Quebec home services businesses

Local Pack & Visibility

Why is my home services business impossible to find on Google even though I have a website and a Google listing?

Having a website is not enough to appear in Google's Local Pack — the block of three businesses with a map displayed first when a client searches "house cleaning Montreal" or "mover near me". That block is driven by your Google Business Profile, not your website. If your listing lacks photos, reviews or the right service category, you do not appear there — and the client calls a competitor before ever learning how reliable you are.

How long does it take for a home services business to appear in Google's local top 3?

Starting from a nearly empty listing, the first visibility gains become noticeable after a few weeks of work on your Google Business Profile. Holding a top-3 spot in a contested area — Montreal, Laval, the South Shore — generally takes several months of steady effort. The pace depends on the number of providers in your service, how often you collect new reviews, and the breadth of your service areas. Our audit gives you a realistic estimate specific to your market.

My listing is already on Google Maps — is that enough to win clients?

Being present on Google Maps is only the starting point. A listing that wins clients includes the right service category, your precise service areas, your insurance or bonding mentions, photos of your team and your work, regular posts, and — most importantly — a steady stream of fresh reviews with thoughtful responses. Most of the listings we audit are barely half-filled, which directly drags down their Local Pack ranking.

Is local SEO really useful for a solo provider or small team?

That is exactly where it pays off most. Local SEO levels the playing field for the small provider against the big franchises, because Google does not reward the size of the company: it values relevance, proximity and local prominence. An independent provider with an optimized listing, recent reviews, well-defined areas and pages by service and neighbourhood can outrank a large outfit that has neglected its local digital presence.

Reviews & Reputation

Can a home services business ask clients for Google Reviews?

Yes, and it is decisive: this sector is not regulated like a licensed profession, so you can freely invite clients to leave a review. The only limit is Google's policy: no fake reviews, no purchased reviews and no incentive. In practice, you ask at the right moment — after a successful job, on the invoice or through an email follow-up — and you respond to every review. When you let someone into a home, trust comes first: a stream of fresh reviews changes everything.

A client left a negative Google review on my business — what should I do?

Never ignore the review and never respond in the heat of the moment. Within a day or two, post a short, professional reply: thank them for the feedback, acknowledge the point raised without a long defence, and offer to resolve the situation directly. A composed reply to a criticism often reassures future clients more than a string of five-star reviews with no responses. We provide response templates adapted to home services.

How many reviews are needed to stand out in the Local Pack?

There is no fixed threshold. Recency matters as much as quantity: a listing with many old reviews is at a disadvantage against a provider that keeps receiving new ones. What counts is a constant flow of credible reviews and systematic responses — all the more important for a trust-based service where you enter people's homes. Our system keeps this rhythm going without weighing down your day.

AI & Technology

Can ChatGPT actually recommend my home services business?

Yes, and it is happening more and more often. When a client asks ChatGPT "a reliable cleaning service on the South Shore", the assistant builds its answer from structured data, directory mentions, articles and Schema.org markup. Providers with complete Schema markup, a rich description of their services and consistent web mentions stand out considerably more often. It is a space most Quebec businesses do not occupy yet.

What is Schema LocalBusiness markup and how is it installed?

It is a JSON-LD markup from Schema.org (type LocalBusiness, or a subtype such as MovingCompany for movers) that describes your business in a structured way for search engines and AI: services, service areas, hours and address. It is installed in your website's HTML and is invisible to visitors. Most Quebec home services websites either lack it or have it incorrectly implemented — making them invisible to generative AI.

Does Quebec's Law 25 apply to my home services business's website?

Yes, as soon as your site collects personal information from Quebec residents — quote request form, appointment booking, online reservation, newsletter sign-up, analytics cookies. Your obligations: an accessible privacy policy, a cookie consent banner, a process for handling access and correction requests, and designating a person responsible for the protection of personal information. Our audit verifies each of these points.

Do I need to rebuild my entire website to improve my local SEO?

Not necessarily. Most local SEO gains do not require a complete rebuild. Google Business Profile optimization, reviews, Schema LocalBusiness markup and NAP citations can all be handled independently of your current site. That said, if your site displays poorly on mobile, loads slowly, does not state your service areas or has no pages by service, a partial or full rebuild becomes worthwhile. Our audit tells you exactly what is priority to maximize your return without unnecessary spending.

How long do local SEO optimizations need to be maintained for a home services business?

Local SEO is not a one-time project — it is ongoing maintenance. Google updates its algorithms regularly, your competitors improve their presence each month, and clients look for recent reviews rather than ones from years ago. We recommend a minimum monthly cadence: Google Business Profile posts, responses to new reviews, updates for holidays and availability, and ranking analysis. Providers that sustain their optimizations consistently dominate their local market over the long term.
