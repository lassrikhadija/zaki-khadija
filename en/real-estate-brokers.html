Frequently asked questions

Frequently asked questions from Quebec real estate brokers

Local Pack & Visibility

Having a website is not enough to appear in Google's Local Pack — the block of three profiles displayed first when an owner searches "real estate broker Rosemont" or "sell my condo Griffintown". That block is driven by your Google Business Profile, not your website. If your listing is incomplete, lacks recent reviews or is poorly categorized, you do not appear there — and the seller calls a better-referenced colleague before ever discovering your sales record.

Starting from a nearly empty listing, the first ranking gains become noticeable after a few weeks of work on your Google Business Profile. Reaching a stable top-3 position in a sought-after area — Plateau, Verdun, Griffintown or the South Shore — generally takes several months of sustained effort. The pace depends on competition in your niche, how regularly you collect new reviews, and the quality of your markup. Our audit gives you a realistic estimate specific to your market.

Being present on Google Maps is only the starting point. A well-kept listing for a broker includes the right category, your areas served, the property types you handle, recent photos of your listings, regular posts, and — most importantly — a steady stream of fresh reviews with thoughtful responses. Most of the listings we audit are barely half-filled, which directly drags down their Local Pack ranking.

That is exactly where it pays off most. Local SEO levels the playing field for the independent broker against large banners, because Google does not reward the size of the brand: it values relevance, proximity and local prominence. A solo broker with an optimized listing, recent reviews and pages by sector and neighbourhood can outrank a large team that has neglected its local digital presence.

Reviews & OACIQ

Yes, provided you follow the OACIQ rules and the Real Estate Brokerage Act regarding advertising and representation. You cannot filter your requests to target only satisfied clients, exchange a benefit for a review, or write fake testimonials. You may, however, invite all of your clients to share their experience after the deed of sale is signed, and provide a direct link. Our method is designed for this framework: non-selective solicitation, no incentive, adapted to regulated professions.

Never ignore the review, and never respond in the heat of the moment or by disclosing details of the transaction. Within a day or two, post a short, composed reply: thank the person, express regret that the experience fell short, and offer to continue the conversation privately. Stay discreet about confidential information tied to the file. A negative review paired with a flawless response often reassures future clients more than a profile with no criticism at all. We provide response templates adapted to the OACIQ framework.

There is no fixed threshold. The recency of reviews matters as much as their number: a profile with many older comments is at a disadvantage against a broker who keeps receiving new ones, even in smaller quantities. What counts is a constant, credible flow, paired with professional responses. Our system maintains this flow within the OACIQ rules, without ongoing effort from you.

AI & Technology

When a buyer asks ChatGPT "which broker to sell a plex in Villeray", the assistant builds its answer from structured data, directory mentions, articles and Schema.org markup. Brokers with complete Schema RealEstateAgent markup, a rich description of their sectors and consistent web mentions stand out considerably more often. It is a space most Quebec brokers do not occupy yet.

Schema RealEstateAgent is a JSON-LD markup from Schema.org that describes your brokerage activity in a structured way for search engines and AI. It states your areas served, the property types you handle (condo, single-family, plex, luxury), your languages, your contact details and your licence number. It is installed in your website's HTML and is invisible to visitors. Most Quebec brokerage websites either lack it or have it incorrectly implemented — making them invisible to generative AI.

Handling sensitive data about your clients' assets, you are particularly concerned. Our audit verifies each of these points.
