Website Launch Guide:
Go Live Without the Stress

Khadija AitLassri May 4, 2026 10 min read

30-Second Summary

  • 301 redirects preserve your SEO equity when changing URLs — required by Google Search Central.
  • 4 critical steps — DNS & propagation, SSL certificate, 301 redirects, functional testing.
  • 5 pre-launch tests — forms, SSL, redirects, mobile (iOS + Android), analytics.
  • Golden rule — never launch on a Friday. Problems don't wait for Monday.

Launching a website is not just a technical act — it's a strategic moment. A poorly executed launch can cost you months of SEO work, interrupt your professional email, and cut your conversion rate in half overnight. Yet with the right checklist, every step is predictable and controllable.

This guide walks you through the 4 critical technical steps, the 5 non-negotiable pre-launch tests, and the mistakes that cost SMBs their organic traffic and clients.

301 redirect — the only type that permanently preserves your SEO equity when changing URLs. Source: Google Search Central.

4 Critical Steps for a Successful Launch

Each of these steps has a direct impact on your visibility and business continuity. None can be skipped:

StepWhat It InvolvesRisk If Skipped
DNS & PropagationPoint your domain to the new serverSite inaccessible for 24–48 h
SSL CertificateActivate HTTPS on all pagesBrowser security warning, loss of trust
301 RedirectsMap old URLs to new ones404 errors, SEO equity lost
Functional TestingVerify forms, links, mobile displayRevenue loss from day one

DNS propagation can take 24 to 48 hours depending on your registrar and the world's DNS servers. Plan your launch during a low-traffic period to minimize client impact during the transition.

5 Non-Negotiable Pre-Launch Tests

Before clicking "go live," run these 5 tests systematically:

  1. Contact forms and conversion forms: fill out each form as a client would. Verify that confirmation emails arrive, that data reaches your CRM, and that the thank-you page displays correctly.
  2. SSL active on all pages: open every page and check the padlock in the address bar. A single non-HTTPS page triggers a browser security warning for all visitors.
  3. 301 redirects in place: test each old URL from your previous site. Every one must redirect to the corresponding new page — not to a generic 404 or to the home page.
  4. Mobile display on iOS Safari and Android Chrome: these two browsers together represent over 90% of mobile traffic. Open each key page on a real device, not just in browser emulation.
  5. Analytics configured and firing: open Google Analytics 4 in real-time mode and navigate through the site. Verify that each page view is tracked and that conversion events are recording correctly.

The True Cost of a Failed Launch

A poorly planned launch has concrete consequences that are sometimes felt for months:

  • Organic traffic loss — missing 301 redirects cause Google to treat new URLs as new pages without history. Your positions built over months can drop to zero overnight.
  • Email interruption — an incorrect MX record configuration during DNS migration can block all incoming email for 24 to 48 hours. Missed client emails, lost opportunities.
  • Conversion rate drop — a broken form, a missing SSL certificate, or a non-functional CTA button on mobile can cut your conversion rate by 50% from the first day.
  • The golden rule: never launch on a Friday. If a problem appears Friday evening, your technical team is unavailable for the weekend. Launch on a Tuesday or Wednesday morning — you have two full working days to resolve any issues.

A launch done right protects everything you've built. Don't risk your SEO rankings and client trust on a checklist item you forgot.
