The gap between your bench and your website

The work is extraordinary. The website makes it look ordinary.

Single-origin chocolate, a small-batch cheese, a hand-poured soap, a roast you dial in by hand — the difference is in the details. But a thin product grid with three small photos and a one-line description tells none of that story. A first-time visitor sees a price, not a reason to choose you over the mass-market shelf.

You rent your audience on social media — you don't own it.

A feed is a wonderful place to be discovered, but the algorithm decides who sees your posts, and a single platform change can cut your reach overnight. Without a site you control, every order, every story and every follower lives somewhere you don't own — and can't be found by someone searching Google for what you make.

Search engines and AI assistants don't know you exist.

When someone types "artisan chocolate gift box Montreal" or asks an assistant for "a local maker of natural soaps", you only appear if your pages are described and structured for them to read. Most independent makers' sites lack that groundwork — so the search ends on someone else's page.

The NEXTIWEB method for artisans and makers

6 building blocks we audit, design and keep working for you

Your story, told well

A homepage and an about page that put your process, your people and your place front and centre. The why behind each batch, the ingredients or materials you choose, the hands that make it — the storytelling that turns a passing visitor into someone who wants this from you.

Photography that sells

Your products presented the way they deserve — generous, well-lit, with real descriptions that say what makes each one different. Guidance on shots that convert, layouts that let the work breathe, and a gallery built to make a mouth water or an eye linger.

Sell your way

A full online shop with shipping, a showcase with click-and-collect, gift cards, pre-orders or seasonal collections — we recommend the model that fits your products, your margins and your time. No forcing you into logistics you don't want.

Found on Google

Pages written around how people actually search — by product, by occasion, by neighbourhood — plus a complete Google Business Profile, the right category, and the on-page basics that let a search for "artisan cheese near me" land on you.

Structured data for AI

Your shop, catalogue and FAQ marked up with Schema.org (Product, LocalBusiness, FAQPage) so ChatGPT, Perplexity and Google AI can read and recommend you. Most independent makers haven't set this up — an open lane to take early.

A list you own

A newsletter sign-up and reviews you control — the audience that comes back for the holiday collection, the new roast, the limited run. Unlike followers on a platform you rent, this is a relationship you keep, fully compliant with Quebec's Law 25.
