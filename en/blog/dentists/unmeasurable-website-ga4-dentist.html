My Site Brings Me Nothing… or Am I Just Not Measuring It?

Khadija AitLassri June 12, 2026 8 min read

30-second summary

  • "Is my site bringing me patients?": without measurement, the answer is impossible — you're flying blind.
  • Traffic isn't patients: what matters are conversions (forms, calls, appointments).
  • GA4 + conversion tracking connect your site to concrete results.
  • Measuring = deciding without guessing, and stopping the waste. Measurement respects Law 25.
The key idea Before concluding that "the site doesn't work," ask the real question: doesn't it work, or are you just not measuring it? Without measurement, you confuse the two — and you make bad decisions.

Many dentists have a site… and a doubt: "is it actually bringing me patients?". Often the problem isn't that the site doesn't work — it's that you have no way of knowing. Here's how to move from impression to proof, without drowning in statistics.

"Is my site bringing me patients?" — the question you shouldn't have to ask

If you can't answer this, it's not a detail: it means you're steering your online presence blind. You don't know what brings patients, nor what's useless — so you can neither improve nor cut. Measurement turns a vague hunch into useful information.

What you don't measure, you can't improve

It's the basic rule. Without data, every decision (rebuild the site, launch an ad, change a text) rests on a guess. With data, you know where to act. Measurement isn't a big-company luxury: it's what keeps you from spending at random and repeating what doesn't work. It's also the natural complement to measuring SEO performance.

Traffic isn't patients: measure what matters

The most common trap: celebrating "lots of visits." But traffic isn't patients. A site can get thousands of visits without generating a single appointment. What matters are conversions — the actions that lead to a new patient:

  • Online appointment requests.
  • Contact forms sent.
  • Clicks on the phone number (calls launched from the site).

That's exactly what turning visitors into appointments aims for — but you still have to measure it.

GA4 and conversion tracking

GA4 (Google Analytics 4) is Google's audience-measurement tool: it shows how people find and use your site. But on its own, it isn't enough — it reports visits, not patients. The real lever is conversion tracking: configuring GA4 to count appointment requests, forms and calls. That's what finally connects your site to concrete results, and lets you see which paths your patients arrive through (Google, the Google listing, advertising…).

Measure to decide (and stop wasting)

Measurement only has value if it informs a decision. A handful of well-chosen indicators beats a cluttered dashboard you never look at. With the right numbers, you know whether an ad pays off, which page converts, where to act first. It's also what sets an honest provider apart: they measure real progress instead of promising miracles.
